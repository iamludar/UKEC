Course Overview

The module delves deeply into the intricate nexus between human resource practices and organisational performance. Students will explore a myriad of techniques, tools and methodologies that top-tier organisations employ to attract, retain and develop talent, ensuring that the workforce remains agile, motivated and aligned with strategic objectives. As we navigate through contemporary challenges such as rapid technological advancements, demographic shifts and a growing emphasis on workplace well-being and work-life balance, this course equips learners with the requisite knowledge to craft and implement people-centric strategies that drive superior performance. Emphasis is placed on understanding the psychology of motivation, the dynamics of team collaboration and the role of leadership in fostering a culture of continuous improvement and innovation. By the end of this module, students will possess a holistic understanding of how tailored people management practices can transform organisational challenges into opportunities, ensuring sustained competitive advantage in a dynamic market.

Learning Outcome & Assessment Criteria

1.  Critically understand the key principles of effective people management and their impact on achieving organisational performance objectives

  1. Analyse the interdependencies between people management strategies and organisational performance indicators
  2. Compare and contrast various people management models and their application in diverse organisational scenarios
  3. Explain key motivational theories and their relevance in modern workplace settings
  4. Evaluate the role of leadership in influencing people management practices and driving performance

‍

2.  Develop competency in crafting and implementing people-centric strategies that drive superior organisational performance

  1. Design tailored people management strategies suitable for specific organisational challenges
  2. Apply strategic human resource techniques to real-world organisational scenarios
  3. Assess the effectiveness of implemented strategies through performance metrics
  4. Reflect critically on the outcomes and iterate the strategies for continuous improvement

‍

3.  Investigate and adapt to modern challenges in people management, considering factors like technological advancements, workforce diversity and shifting employee needs

  1. Research current trends and disruptions in people management due to technological evolution
  2. Discuss the implications of a globally diverse workforce on people management strategies
  3. Identify modern employee expectations and devise strategies to meet them
  4. Collaborate with colleagues to brainstorm innovative solutions to emergent people management challenges

‍

4.  Prioritise continuous improvement, feedback mechanisms and adaptability in crafting and refining people management practices for sustained performance

  1. Establish systematic feedback loops to continually assess the effectiveness of people management initiatives
  2. Recommend iterative improvements based on gathered feedback and changing organisational landscapes
  3. Monitor shifts in organisational objectives and realign people management practices accordingly
  4. Validate the effectiveness of adapted strategies through periodic reviews and stakeholder feedback

5.  Effectively communicate, present and advocate for tailored people management strategies to diverse stakeholders, ensuring buy-in and collaborative implementation

  1. Communicate the rationale and benefits of proposed people management strategies to stakeholders
  2. Present data-driven findings and recommendations persuasively to organisational decision-makers
  3. Collaborate with cross-functional teams to ensure cohesive strategy execution
  4. Negotiate with potential resistors, ensuring alignment and commitment to the proposed strategies
