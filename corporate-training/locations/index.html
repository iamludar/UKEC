Our role

UKEC helps make LIBT programmes clearer, closer, and easier to access across Asia-Pacific.

We are not simply listing training locations. This page is the cooperation entry point for regional partners who need a structured way to connect students, institutions, and organisations with LIBT pathways.

01

Regional admissions coordination

Initial enquiry handling, document preparation guidance, programme matching, and communication before formal submission.

02

Partner channel development

Support for schools, colleges, agencies, and institutions that want a responsible regional route into LIBT opportunities.

03

Project and training cooperation

Discussion support for corporate learning, professional development, short courses, and institution-led education projects.

Cooperation models

Ways education institutions can work with UKEC.

We welcome serious project conversations with partners who can support quality learner engagement, local delivery readiness, student services, or market access.

Student progression channels

Build enquiry and progression routes for students interested in LIBT-linked diploma, top-up, postgraduate, and professional pathways.

Institutional project cooperation

Discuss co-branded information sessions, pathway briefings, regional recruitment plans, and programme positioning.

Corporate training enquiries

Coordinate professional learning needs from companies, industry associations, and employer-sponsored learner groups.

Local learner support

Work with partners who can provide reliable consultation, document collection, language support, and learner follow-up.

Regional market development

Event and seminar collaboration

Host online or offline briefings for students, parents, counsellors, institutions, and corporate learning teams.

Process

A simple cooperation path before any formal commitment.

We start with fit and responsibility first, then move into programme mapping and a practical cooperation plan.

1

Initial discussion

Share your organisation profile, region, learner base, and the type of cooperation you want to explore.

2

Fit mapping

UKEC reviews whether LIBT-related pathways or professional projects match your audience and service capacity.

3

Project proposal

We define the programme focus, communication responsibilities, lead handling, and operational expectations.

4

Launch support

Approved cooperation can move into briefings, materials coordination, enquiry handling, and ongoing partner support.

Partner standards

Accuracy, transparency, and student-first communication.

All programme information, admissions details, and project commitments must remain aligned with LIBT confirmation. UKEC supports regional partners, but we do not encourage over-promising, misleading promotion, or unclear learner expectations.

Next step

Tell us what kind of cooperation you want to build.

Send your organisation background, target market, learner profile, and preferred cooperation model. UKEC will help assess whether there is a suitable path forward.
