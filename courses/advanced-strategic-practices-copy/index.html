Programme Overview

Advanced Strategic Practices is a two-day executive training tailored for senior leaders, including elected officials, C-suite executives, department heads, and team leads from diverse sectors such as business, security, hospitality, education, and public sector agencies.

Most high-performing managers reach a ceiling, not because they lack capability, but because they are operating without the frameworks that convert tactical effort into strategic leverage.

This programme provides those frameworks, drawn from the most significant bodies of management research of the last fifty years, and applies them directly to each participant’s live department.

Participants leave with a documented Strategic Operating System - a decision architecture they can deploy from the morning after the programme ends.

Business Value

The failure to elevate above daily operations leads to “Strategic Drift” and missed market opportunities. McKinsey research shows that organisations with high decision-making velocity and quality generate 2.5x higher growth, 2x higher profit, and 30% higher return on invested capital. Yet only 37% of organisations make decisions that are both high-quality and fast. The cost of slow, poor-quality decision-making is estimated at 10–15% of annual revenue through organisational friction, missed opportunities, and leadership bottlenecks. (McKinsey 2024; Harvard Business Review 2023).
This programme is a Velocity Multiplier. By shifting the leader's focus from “Tasks” to “Systems”, the organisation accelerates its “Intent-to-Execution” cycle. This protects EBITDA by ensuring the department is agile enough to pivot based on data, not habit.

Intended Outcomes

Diagnose and dismantle the structural barriers preventing strategic work.
Design a high-velocity decision architecture that accelerates execution without sacrificing quality.
Build probabilistic forecasting capability to navigate uncertainty with calibrated confidence.
Master executive communication using evidence-based influence techniques.
Reduce measurable leadership debt and reclaim 5+ hours weekly for strategic work.

Curriculum

Day 1 - Session 1
The Leverage Ratio
Identifying the 20% of leadership activities driving 80% of results. Participants conduct a “Time Audit” to quantify current tactical vs. strategic allocation, revealing where managerial energy is wasted on low-leverage activities.
System Mapping
Mapping your department's “Stocks and Flows”. Understanding feedback loops, delays, and unintended consequences in organisational systems.
Theory of Constraints
Pinpointing the primary bottleneck holding back team velocity. Understanding that optimising non-constraints wastes effort and strategic leaders focus resources on the limiting step that determines total system output.
Day 1 - Session 2
High-Velocity Decision Models
Mastering Amazon's “Type 1 vs. Type 2 Decision” framework. Categorising decisions by reversibility to apply appropriate rigour and speed.
Second-Order Thinking
The “And Then What?” protocol for risk mitigation. Evaluating not just immediate effects but downstream, systemic consequences 12-24 months out.
Bias Auditing
Using AI tools to “red-team” a live strategic proposal. Identifying cognitive biases, logical gaps, and hidden assumptions in your own thinking.
Day 2 - Session 3
Weak Signal Detection
Differentiating between “Market Noise” and “Structural Trend”. Building a personal scanning system for early warning indicators. Participants identify three weak signals in their industry and assess probabilities of impact.
Scenario Mapping
Building a 12-month “Option-Based” strategy for Best/Worst/Probable outcomes. Creating strategic flexibility through scenario planning rather than single-point forecasts.
Superforecasting Protocols
Understand how to increase prediction accuracy. Understanding base rates before claiming uniqueness, updating beliefs incrementally with new evidence, and calibrating confidence.
Day 2 - Session 4
Strategic Synthesis
Structuring complex ideas into “Idea-First” narratives using the SCQA framework. Converting operational data into high-gravity, boardroom-ready proposals. Understanding pyramid logic: lead with the answer, support with MECE-grouped arguments.
Influencing Without Authority
Securing cross-functional resources in matrixed environments. Building coalitions and navigating organisational politics ethically. Understanding the difference between persuasion and manipulation).
The SOS Presentation
Defending your 90-day “Strategic Operating System” plan against a simulated Board challenge. Participants present their department’s strategic diagnosis, guiding policy, and coherent action. Live practice with peer feedback and facilitator coaching.

30-Day Implementation Bridge

Week 1

Deploy the first decision protocol change (Type 1/Type 2 framework); begin tracking decision velocity baseline using leading indicators identified in Lesson 1.1.

Week 2

Complete the Second-Order Decision Log for three live strategic choices using the “And Then What?” protocol from Lesson 2.2. Document downstream effects and adjust decisions accordingly.

Week 3

Present the SOS framework to your direct reports; gather feedback on diagnosis accuracy and guiding policy feasibility. Iterate based on input, refining coherent actions.

Week 4

Attend the “Shift Audit” clinic with UKEC facilitator to verify implementation progress and troubleshoot barriers. Review updated system maps, scenario plans, and decision velocity metrics. Final checkpoint before certification submission.

Frequently Asked Questions
I'm already a successful manager. Why do I need this programme?
Success as a manager often creates a trap: your competence at solving problems makes you the department's bottleneck. This programme helps you transition from being the best problem-solver to being the architect of systems that solve problems without you. McKinsey research shows that organisations with fast, high-quality decision-making generate 2.5x higher growth. This programme teaches you to build that capability into your team.
How is this different from a typical leadership course?
Most leadership programmes focus on soft skills or inspirational content. This is a “systems engineering” programme for leaders. You’ll leave with tangible artefacts: a documented Strategic Operating System, a decision architecture, and a 90-day implementation roadmap. The 30-Day Bridge ensures you don't just learn theory but that you implement it.
I don't have time for a 2-day programme. I’m too busy firefighting.
That’s precisely why you need this programme. We’ll show you that “firefighting” is often a symptom of poor decision architecture, not insufficient effort. Participants typically identify 5+ hours per week of time that can be reclaimed through better delegation and decision protocols. The investment of 12 hours now prevents hundreds of hours of tactical churn later.
Will this help me get promoted to Director/VP level?
Explicitly yes. The programme addresses the most common barrier to senior promotion: the inability to demonstrate strategic thinking whilst trapped in operational delivery. The SOS Playbook and Minto Executive Brief become portfolio pieces that evidence your readiness for the next level.
What tools will I learn to use?
You’ll gain hands-on experience with Miro/Lucidchart for system mapping, AI tools (Claude / Gemini) for strategic “red-teaming,” and dashboard design principles for Looker Studio. All tools are platform-agnostic allowing the skills transfer regardless of your organisation's specific tech stack.
